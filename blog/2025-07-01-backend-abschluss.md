July 2025. My backend certificate.

I was grateful. Truly. This training gave me so much —
APIs, databases, authentication — everything that powers a website
behind the scenes, invisible but essential.

And then I faced a question.

![Backend Certificate](/img/blog/2025/20250903_172203.jpg)

{/* truncate */}

---

I started writing my CV. Wanted to apply. Get ready.

But something didn't feel finished.

I could write clean frontend code. I could build backends.
But I didn't know how to **properly publish** what I was building.
How to ensure security. How to set up a server that doesn't
collapse at the first attack. How to automate deployments
so you can focus on what actually matters.

There was still a gap. And I couldn't ignore it.

## My Projects from the Backend Course
![Backend Projects](/img/blog/2025/backend.png)

---

Around that time, the Developer Akademie introduced a new training program:
**DevSecOps**.

My contract had just ended and I realized there was still one critical piece
I didn't fully understand: clean deployment, automation and security.

I knew that was exactly where my next step lay.

In the process, I had the opportunity to apply for a government education voucher
to cover the training costs.

I submitted the application and two weeks later the approval came through.

I was incredibly happy. Not just because of the voucher, but because I knew:
Now I get to learn the final piece. Now I'll truly understand the whole system.

It started right away.
